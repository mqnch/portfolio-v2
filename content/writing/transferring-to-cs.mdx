## Some background stuff

One year ago, I was making my university decisions. I was hung up on whether to choose CS/SE @ McGill or Math @ UWaterloo.

On one hand, I would've had a great time at McGill doing anything but work, but I'm glad I chose UWaterloo, despite the "risk" of not transferring and staying in math. 

If someone were in my position a year ago, I think it'd be important to understand that transferring to CS isn't guaranteed, and also matters far less than you might think. 

UWaterloo is so great because of the flexible and abundant co-op terms (and the culture), so it's really up to how much effort you're putting in, not your major.

While I am ecstatic, I believe that transferring to CS won't significantly improve my career opportunities. It does almost double my tuition though.

## Alright cool, but what was my CS average and MAV?

My grades were straight butt. If you want to see the grades of everyone that transferred (or didn't), someone made a <a href="https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1b2nHaNYUviOOC3i0D3hWjMoPyEt1lWmi9V-0qWW5yjc/edit?gid=1648921553#gid=1648921553" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" className="font-mono text-gray-300 underline decoration-gray-600 underline-offset-4 hover:text-white hover:decoration-gray-400">spreadsheet</a> for S26.

I ended CS136 with an 89, CS145 with an 80, and my MAV was 81.5. Some butt ass grades. Also, I'd say my application writing was pretty passable. 

Off-topic but I think CS145 is really worth taking, since it was super fun (until lambda calculus) and changed the way I view computation, but I straight FAILED that midterm. Also I think they curved our grades down??

I was pretty surprised I transferred since our CS136 final was so easy and the course average was probably high.

Here's some advice on transferring passed down to me from many upper years, but take it with a grain of salt since none of this is 100% confirmed:
- They primarily care about your main CS course grades (ex. CS136, CS245)
- The application writing portion doesn't matter
- They look at your MAV grade and if you took advanced courses as a tiebreaker
